Flight Cancellation Analysis¶

Dataset Overview¶

These are six dataset downloaded from ASA website (http://stat-computing.org/dataexpo/2009/the-data.html). They contain flight cancellation information from 2003 to 2008. I will use these dataset to analyze the reasons for cancellation and which airport caused the most of the cancellation.¶

Approach: I decide to explore the data in 2008 first. Then I will cross analyze the data from 2003 to 2008.¶

Observation 1: 137434 out of 7009728 flights were cancelled, 2% of the total flights in 2008.¶

Observation 9: Both United and American decreased the total number of outbound flights from O’Hare airport since 2003. However, Envoy and SkyWest increased the number flights. SkyWest increased the number flights for nearly 117 times.¶

Observation 10: the percentage of NAS-cancellation rate, which did not change much for United and American. The rate increased for Envoy. The rate for SkyWest was the highest in 2003, mainly because there were only around 400 flights in that year. It dropped dramatically in 2004 but has climbed up steadily since 2005.¶

Observation 11: Overall, Envoy and SkyWest had higher NAS-cancellation rate than United and American. The fact that the NAS-cancellation rate of United and American had not had much change indicates O’Hare maintains NAS-scheduling priority for United and American, two of the mainline carriers in the U.S., over smaller carriers.¶
